Sony WH-1000XM4 — In-Depth Review

A practical look at Sony's long-running powerhouse of noise-cancelling headphones.

By TechWave Reviews • March 2026 • Reviews

The Sony WH-1000XM4 remain one of the most balanced and user-friendly noise-cancelling headphones on the market. They blend excellent active noise cancellation (ANC), warm and detailed sound, and long battery life in a comfortable, lightweight package. After spending a few days with them, here's how they perform in real-world use.

Design & Comfort

Sony's design hasn't chased flashy looks — instead it's quietly refined. The WH-1000XM4 are well-padded, fold compactly for travel, and the clamping force is relaxed enough for multi-hour listening sessions. The breathable earpads and light headband make them comfortable whether you're commuting, working from a cafe, or editing at home.

Noise Cancellation

The ANC on the WH-1000XM4 is class-leading for everyday environments: it tames constant city noise, airplane rumble, and the hum of office ventilation very effectively. Sony's adaptive features tune the cancellation to your activity and surroundings, and in practice the result is a significantly quieter listening bubble without odd artifacts.

Sound Quality

Sonically, the XM4s provide a warm, engaging signature with strong midrange presence and enough bass to satisfy most listeners without overwhelming clarity. High frequencies are smooth and never harsh. For the casual listener and for podcasts, meetings, and cinematic playback, the balance is excellent. Audiophiles may want a flatter profile, but the Sony app's EQ lets you adjust the tuning easily.

Battery & Connectivity

Battery life is excellent — you can expect well over 20 hours of playback with ANC active in typical use. Pairing is seamless via Bluetooth, multipoint support works well for switching between phone and laptop, and the onboard controls are intuitive. Quick charging is also helpful for travel days.

Extras

Sony packs useful extras: a companion app with EQ and ANC controls, a speak-to-chat feature that pauses playback when you start talking, and reliable call quality for meetings. They don't feel cluttered with gimmicks — the extras add real convenience.

Verdict

If you want comfortable, excellent-sounding headphones with one of the best ANC systems available, the Sony WH-1000XM4 are still a top choice. They strike a compelling balance between sound, noise cancellation, battery life, and comfort. For frequent travelers and remote workers, they're an easy recommendation. I score them 4.5 out of 5 — a small step below the newest flagship models in absolute technical detail, but better in everyday ease-of-use and value.
